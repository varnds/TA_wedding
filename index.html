Anand’s Serendipitous First Date in San Francisco

For two years, Anand had been living the dream, or at least a version of it involving a backpack, no fixed address, and a blissful disregard for anything resembling a plan. He was a man of the road, a wanderer, a serial city-hopper who had turned “just passing through” into something of a lifestyle.

Meanwhile, across the world (and across every conceivable lifestyle), Tiuli was doing the opposite. After years in consulting, hopping cities and living out of suitcases, she had finally put down roots in San Francisco. She had an apartment. She had a routine. She had, blessedly, unpacked her bags.

Then Anand decided to swing through San Francisco for just one month to meet his parents during their travels from Chennai. One month. That’s it. No big deal. Except, it happened to be the same city, the same month, and through a serendipitous swipe on a dating app — the same universe as Tiuli.

They matched. They agreed to meet. And so Anand, freshly arrived and entirely unfamiliar with the city, did what any self-respecting first dater would do: he went for a stroll to find the perfect spot. He walked. He explored. He took his sweet time.

He found a coffee shop with an absolutely stunning view of the Golden Gate Bridge. Expansive windows. Good vibes. Totally charming. He was very proud of himself.

What he had not noticed — and could not have noticed in his defense — was that this coffee shop was attached to the back of a seedy hostel. Like, attached attached. Tiuli arrived. She took one look around. She raised an eyebrow. She said nothing.

She stayed anyway. (This, if you ask Anand, is the moment he knew she was extraordinary.)

What followed was six hours of conversation, laughter, and the slow, delightful realization that two people who had lived such wildly different lives had somehow orbited the same places. They wandered through Golden Gate Park, rented a rowboat on Stow Lake, argued (gently) about books, and discovered they had more in common than either had any right to expect.

To this day, Anand is genuinely, deeply relieved that the whole hostel situation didn’t send Tiuli running, because that slightly sketchy coffee shop, that improbable first date, turned out to be the beginning of something he hadn’t known he was looking for.

And to this day, Tiuli is so grateful that, for once, her (often reckless) fearlessness led her to exactly the right person she had always been looking for.

Some people spend years searching. Anand and Tiuli needed exactly one month, one walk, and one questionable first date recommendation to find the love of their lives.

Brunch

Brunch at Piccino Presidio

1 Letterman Drive, Building D, San Francisco, CA 94129

Rated a top 20 SF brunch spot by The Infatuation. If you’re heading out for a morning stroll through the Presidio, make Piccino your first stop. It might not be your first SF farm-to-table restaurant, but it may just become your favorite. Floor-to-ceiling windows flood the space with light, and the relaxed, airy room makes it easy to settle in and stay a while. The strawberry-chantilly french toast is a must-order — but yes, always get the pizza with an egg on top, and the spring green garlic with anchovy and chili oil is the move.

There’s usually two-hour street parking in the area. The block of Lyon Street that runs parallel to the park, directly outside the restaurant, generally offers two-hour spaces.

View

Stroll Along the Presidio Tunnel Tops & Golden Gate Views

210 Lincoln Blvd, San Francisco, CA 94129

Just a 5 minute walk from our reception venue, the Golden Gate Club, Presidio Tunnel Tops offers some of the most panoramic and iconic views in all of San Francisco, overlooking the Golden Gate Bridge, Alcatraz, the Bay, and Crissy Field. You can spend the morning strolling through beautifully landscaped parks, relaxing in picnic areas throughout the grounds, and exploring the Presidio’s historic former Army homes and buildings that give the area its unique charm and character.

On the weekends, the park also hosts a solid lineup of rotating food trucks, making it an easy spot to grab lunch, coffee, or snacks before getting ready for the reception!
